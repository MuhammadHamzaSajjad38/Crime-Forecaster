📊 Data Insights

Exploring the patterns and trends within the Chicago crime dataset.

Top 15 Most Frequent Crime Types

The analysis reveals that "Theft" is by far the most common crime category, followed by "Battery" and "Criminal Damage." This highlights the prevalence of property and personal contact crimes in the dataset.

Temporal Crime Patterns

Crimes by Hour of the Day

Crime incidents peak in the evening hours, particularly between 6 PM and 10 PM. A significant drop occurs in the early morning, with the lowest activity around 5 AM, before rising again during the day.

Crimes by Month

There is a clear seasonal trend, with crime rates increasing during the warmer summer months (May to August) and decreasing during the colder winter months. This is a common pattern in urban environments.

Context of Crime Incidents

Arrest vs. Non-Arrest Crimes

A significant majority of reported crimes do not result in an immediate arrest, indicating the challenges in law enforcement response and case clearance.

Domestic vs. Non-Domestic Crimes

Approximately 12.8% of crimes in the dataset are classified as domestic incidents. While a minority, this still represents a substantial number of cases occurring in residential settings.
